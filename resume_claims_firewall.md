# Resume Claims Firewall

This document establishes the hard truth boundary. The agent and any tailored resumes must strictly obey these boundaries. If a claim sounds stronger than the evidence in the repo, downgrade it.

## A. Approved Claims

* **Nexus WiFi:** Remote technical support for 150+ managed properties. 1,500+ hours of remote customer-facing support. Triage of access points, switches, gateways, and modems.
* **Impulse (WhisperLocal):** GPU-accelerated speech-to-text dictation application for Windows using Python, OpenAI Whisper, and NVIDIA CUDA. Fully built application published as a public beta.
* **Live Pilot:** Functional prototype of a voice-controlled assistant for Ableton Live using Python, Google Gemini API, and OSC. Contains a 6-agent architecture and a deterministic execution pipeline reducing API calls from 30+ to 1.
* **Certifications:** Google IT Support Certification (Coursera, Nov 2022).

## B. Cautious Claims

* **Midway Auto Group Inventory System:** *Claiming:* "Built and maintained a spreadsheet-based inventory tracking system... Improved visibility and organization." *Why cautious:* Do not imply it caused massive sales growth; it was administrative/operational support. Keep attribution honest.
* **Location/Relocation:** *Claiming:* Open to remote or Austin, TX. *Why cautious:* Relocation requires ~2 months; do not present as a local Austin candidate ready to start in-office tomorrow. 

## C. Banned Claims

* **Banned Wording:** "Bachelor of Science in IT", "Bachelor of Business Commerce", "Graduated from Miami University"
  * *Why Risk:* Absolute loss of credibility during background check. Falsification of credentials.
* **Banned Wording:** "Network Engineer", "Advanced Protocol Routing", "Infrastructure Monitoring and Incident Response"
  * *Why Risk:* Nexus WiFi experience is tier 1/2 device triage, not network engineering. No specific formal monitoring tools were used. Interviewers will instantly detect the gap.
* **Banned Wording:** "SLA-focused", "99% SLA Adherence"
  * *Why Risk:* There are no confirmed SLA metrics available in the work history. Making them up fails the truth standard.
* **Banned Wording:** "Implemented company policies, technical procedures, and standards for data security"
  * *Why Risk:* Meaningless fluff text that was duplicated across roles. It adds zero value and reduces scan credibility.
* **Banned Wording:** "Enterprise-grade production software" (regarding Live Pilot / Impulse)
  * *Why Risk:* Live Pilot is a functional prototype. Impulse is an independent beta app. Framing them as enterprise production hides the fact that they were solo projects.

## D. Approved Wording Rules

This section dictates the exact translation limits and framing that are allowed.

### Education Wording
**Rule:** Only use this exact phrasing. Do not add degrees. Do not claim graduation.
**Approved:** 
> Miami University — Business Management Technology coursework
> Completed 67 credit hours toward Associate of Applied Business
> Attended through Spring 2024

### Nexus WiFi Framing
**Rule:** Frame exclusively as foundational-to-intermediate technical support. 
**Approved:** "Diagnose and triage issues with access points, switches, gateways, and modems, coordinating hardware replacement when needed."
**Limit:** Do not imply protocol-level troubleshooting (deep DNS, VPN). Do not inflate to "Systems Engineering".

### Impulse (WhisperLocal) Framing
**Rule:** Frame as a shipped beta product, but explicitly attribute it as an independent project showing full lifecycle.
**Approved:** "Built and shipped a GPU-accelerated speech-to-text dictation app for Windows using Python and OpenAI Whisper, published as a public beta on GitHub."
**Limit:** Do not claim it is actively used by enterprise clients or that it is highly stable.

### Live Pilot Framing
**Rule:** Frame as a functional prototype that demonstrates system architecture and API integration.
**Approved:** "Built a voice-controlled AI DAW assistant (functional prototype) utilizing a 6-agent architecture and deterministic execution pipeline."
**Limit:** Do not refer to it as a released B2B/B2C product.

### Metrics Usage
**Rule:** Only use explicitly verified metrics.
**Approved metrics:** "150+ managed properties", "1,500+ hours of customer-facing support", "reduction of API calls from 30+ to 1" (for Live Pilot), "2-10 resolved tickets per shift".
**Limit:** Do not invent metrics for performance speeds, user adoption numbers for side projects, or sales growth.

### Project-to-Role Translation Limits
**Rule:** Projects can be used as evidence of technical *ability*, but they cannot be translated into professional *experience* on an enterprise team.
**Limit:** Do not write "Lead Developer" or "Software Engineer" as a formal job title based on these projects. They reside firmly in the "Projects" section of the resume.
