## Top 5 Credibility Risks in Current Materials

*Fact vs. Interpretation:* These risks are based on discrepancies and padding identified in previous iterations of the resume.

1. **Degree Inflation**
   *Fact:* Previous resumes used variations like "Bachelor of Science in IT" or "BS in Business." 
   *Fact:* Isaiah completed 67 credit hours toward an Associate's degree and withdrew.
   *Risk:* Total loss of credibility if asked for a degree verification during a background check.
   *Source:* `01_Master_Profile/Master_Candidate_Profile.md` (Lines 126-127, 224)

2. **Overstated Title & Enterprise Scope (Nexus WiFi)**
   *Fact:* The role is Network Support Technician, primarily dealing with downed APs/modems and tier-1/tier-2 device support.
   *Risk:* Framing the role as advanced network engineering (protocol-level troubleshooting like deep DNS/VPN routing) will quickly unravel in a technical interview with heavy network engineers.
   *Source:* `01_Master_Profile/Master_Candidate_Profile.md` (Lines 45, 57-58)

3. **Fabricated SLA / Performance Metrics**
   *Fact:* No formal SLA metrics or monitoring tools have been verified. Ticket volume ranges from 2-10 per shift.
   *Risk:* Throwing out percentages or strict SLA adherence metrics without evidence will sound like generic fluff or lead to interview questions that cannot be confidently answered.
   *Source:* `01_Master_Profile/Master_Candidate_Profile.md` (Lines 59, 193-194)

4. **Misrepresenting Personal Projects as Enterprise Products**
   *Fact:* Live Pilot is a functional prototype. Impulse is a beta release with edge-case instability.
   *Risk:* Masking these as deployed enterprise-grade SaaS products with active users will backfire under architecture and scaling scrutiny in SE interviews.
   *Source:* `01_Master_Profile/Master_Candidate_Profile.md` (Lines 144, 160-161, 171)

5. **Fluffy, Meaningless Summary Wording**
   *Interpretation:* Using terms like "results-driven" or "implemented company policies" is filler that occupies valuable vertical real estate without adding verifiable proof.
   *Fact:* Existing "Key Accounts Team Assistant" role included duplicate filler bullets.
   *Source:* `01_Master_Profile/Master_Candidate_Profile.md` (Lines 69-70)

## What is Currently Working

* **Quantifiable Remote Support Volume:** The 1,500+ hours and 150+ properties metrics are real, defensible, and show massive repetition and reliability.
* **Demonstrable Technical Buildership:** Public GitHub repositories with well-documented code (Live Pilot, WhisperLocal) serve as indisputable proof of Python, API integration, and system design skills.
* **Modular Strategy:** Having separate lane-specific templates already forces the narrative to shift naturally rather than trying to stuff everything into a single unfocused resume.
* *Sources for all:* `01_Master_Profile/Master_Candidate_Profile.md`, `job_search_system_plan.md`

## What Must Change Immediately

* **Eradicate all remaining generic fluff.** If a sentence doesn't contain a metric, a specific technology, or a verifiable outcome, kill it.
* **Institutionalize the "Claims Firewall."** Every tailored resume moving forward must strictly map to the exact approved phrases for education and project scopes to prevent LLM hallucination and keyword stuffing.
* **Format for Skimmability.** Recruiters need to see the technical tools and support metrics inside of 6 seconds. Dense walls of text must be converted to punchy facts.
