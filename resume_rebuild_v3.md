### TSE Resume Notes
* **Why the section order works:** Summary, Skills, Experience, Projects, Education. This is the orthodox sequence expected by ATS software and human recruiters. It establishes immediate credibility through 4 years of relevant support experience before bringing in the tech projects as a bonus.
* **3 Strongest Bullets:**
  1. "Provide remote technical troubleshooting for external customers across 150+ managed properties." (Establishes massive, unfalsifiable business scope)
  2. "Triage connectivity outages and conduct fault isolation across access points, switches..." (Direct integration of hard tech keywords within context)
  3. "Maintain continuous outbound communication with non-technical end users..." (Highlights the crucial soft skills at the core of all Support Engineering roles)
* **3 Weakest Remaining Gaps:**
  1. No specific Tier-3 infrastructure networking tools commonly found in massive enterprise environments (e.g., Zabbix, Azure Active Directory).
  2. Brief tenure in the insurance role limits the narrative impact of corporate operations outside of Nexus WiFi.
  3. "Coursework" rather than a completed degree.
* **3 Likely Recruiter Objections:**
  1. "Does he have enough deep networking (Layer 2/3 routing) knowledge for an escalation role?"
  2. "Will he need immediate relocation support to move to Austin?"
  3. "Does 'Network Support Technician' translate to 'Support Engineer' at a SaaS company?"

* **Why the section order works:** Summary, Skills, Experience, Projects, Education. While his technical projects are the *reason* he qualifies for SE roles, placing them before his Experience violates ATS parsing norms and highlights the fact that his formal title is not "Solutions Engineer". Sticking to the standard format allows the Resume Summary and Skills block to sell the technical depth *before* they digest the Experience.
* **3 Strongest Bullets:**
  1. "Designed a deterministic multi-agent pipeline... compressing API calls per execution to a single operation." (Pure architecture and optimization demonstration)
  2. "Conduct 1,500+ hours of remote customer-facing support, bridging technical fault isolation... with accessible guidance." (Shows the essential dual-nature 'bridge' needed for SE roles)
  3. "Managed end-to-end product implementation..." (Proves holistic understanding of a software delivery lifecycle)
* **3 Weakest Remaining Gaps:**
  1. The projects, while highly impressive, are solo builds. He lacks B2B enterprise code deployment experience.
  2. The current formal job title ("Support Technician") does not strictly mandate programming in his day-to-day duties.
  3. No completed bachelor’s degree in computer science or related field.
* **3 Likely Recruiter Objections:**
  1. "He can code his own projects, but has he ever implemented a technical solution for an actively paying B2B client?"
  2. "Why is a Support Technician applying for an Implementation/Solutions Engineering role?"
